History

    The time we began!

    The first one!

    The first one is always special! So was SPRINT'08. We had 12 categories of events including athletics, cricket, football, badminton among other sports.

Swimming

Will the waters consume you?

Men's 100mtrs Freestyle | Women's 100mtrs Freestyle | Men's 50mtrs Backstroke | Women's 50mtrs Backstroke | 4x50mtrs Freestyle Relay

Rules

* Every player is requested to carry their valid college ID cards. Entry and participation will not be permitted without it *

Freestyle

Freestyle means any style other than backstroke, breaststroke or butterfly.

Some part of the swimmer must touch the wall upon completion of each length and at the finish.

Some part of the swimmer must break the surface of the water throughout the race, except it shall be permissible for the swimmer to be completely submerged during the turn and for a distance of not more than 15 metres after the start and each turn. By that point, the head must have broken the surface.

Backstroke

Prior to the starting signal, the swimmers shall line up in the water facing the starting end, with both hands holding the starting grips. Standing in or on the gutter or bending the toes over the lip of the gutter is prohibited.

Upon the finish of the race the swimmer must touch the wall while on the back in his/her respective lane.

Relay

For the 4X50 metres freestyle teams of 4 have to be formed. All the 4 participants must be from the same college. Boys and girls can form a mixed team.

If the second, third or 4th swimmer starts before the swimmer prior to him touches the wall in his/her respective lane, the entire team will automatically be disqualified.

Throwball

Throw To Win

Rules

* Every player is requested to carry their valid college ID cards. Entry and participation will not be permitted without it *

Each team should consist of 7 players at time of play.

The team shall consist of 7 active players and 3 substitutes.

Any ball to be released should be released from above the shoulder/shoulder-line only.

The ball has to be caught with both the hands and returned in one hand only.

Service ball should not touch the net.

Double touch is not allowed for the service ball.

The ball should be served after the whistle & within 5 seconds.

Serve the ball only from the service- -zone & without crossing the end line.

Any ball falling on the box line or in the dead zone is a foul.

Any ball after catching (during rally) should be released within 3 seconds.

During the rally, ball can touch the net.

Body touch- during catching or releasing the ball should not touch any part of the body except the palm.

Dubs- while catching, the ball should be caught simultaneously with both the hands.

Two players cannot catch the ball simultaneously.

Any ball (service/rally) falling on the side-line or the end-line is a good ball.

The players should stay in 3-1-3 position at the time of service.

Shifting the ball from right to left or left to right is not permitted.

Pushing the ball deliberately is not permitted.

The service ball or the rally ball should not touch the antenna.

Two time-outs of (30 seconds each) are permitted for each set.

The format will be knockout or round robin based on number of participating teams. The referee’s decision will be deemed final in any case of controversy and that will stand above any challenge by any team.

Cricket

Lord Of The Games!

League | Event is currently open for men only

Rules

* Every player is requested to carry their valid college ID cards. Entry and participation will not be permitted without it *

Please note that matches are starting January 18, 2015

All the games would be played with red leather balls.

The dress code is strictly white T shirts and trousers. A player will not be allowed if he wears an apparel of any other colour.

The teams have to carry their own kit bag to the matches.

The teams have to abide by the schedule. Failure of a team to turn up for the match 15 mins before the scheduled time is liable to be disqualified. No consideration will be entertained.

The pre-quarter final and quarter final matches will be of 12 overs a side. Semi-final and final would be of 15 overs a side.

Team shall comprise of 11 players + 3 substitutes. Team names have to be given to the match coordinator and the umpires before the toss.

POWER PLAY OVERS

For 12 overs a side game, first 4 overs would be the powerplay overs where only 2 players are allowed outside the inner circle.

For 15 overs a side game, initial 4 overs would be the powerplay overs followed by a 1 over batting powerplay in which the batting side has the discretion to take the power play at any time of the innings.

For non powerplay overs, a minimum of 4 fielders has to be within the inner circle.

BOWLING RESTRICTION

For a 12 over game, 2 bowlers can bowl a maximum of 3 overs and others can bowl a maximum of 2 overs.

For a 15 over game, a bowler can bowl a maximum of 3 overs.

OTHER IMPORTANT RULES

If a bowler oversteps, there would be a free hit of the next ball. The batsman can only get out if there is a run out.

1 bouncer per over is allowed.

If a player is injured, a runner will not be allowed. He will have to be retired hurt.

In case there is a tie in the match, there would be a super over where the team batting second in the actual match will bat first. Standard rules of a super over apply. If the result of a super over is again a tie, the team which has scored the maximum sixes in the original match would be declared the winner. If that is also same, number of fours in the original match will be looked at. If even then a result can’t be drawn, the team which has taken the maximum wickets will be adjudged the winner.

For a 12 over match, the max time for a team to bowl their overs is 1 hr 10 mins and for a 15 over match, the max time for a team to bowl is 1 hr 30 mins. Time between two innings would be 15 mins. If the fielding team exceeds the time limit, for every over bowled beyond the time limit, 5 runs per over will be deducted. Any incidents such as loss of ball or player injury will be excluded from the time duration.

In case a ball is lost, another ball of similar condition will be provided which will be chosen by the umpires.

During the game, except the players, umpires and event coordinators, no one would be allowed to enter the playing area.

Other standard cricket rules apply.

The umpire’s decision will be final and all players have to abide by it.

Frisbee

Going Around In Circles!

This is an informal event

Rules

* Every player is requested to carry their valid college ID cards. Entry and participation will not be permitted without it *

Each point begins with both teams lining up on the front of their respective endzone line. The defense throws the disc to the offense. A regulation game has seven players per team.

Each time the offense completes a pass in the defense's endzone, the offense scores a point. Play is initiated after each score

The disc may be advanced in any direction by completing a pass to a teammate. Players may not run with the disc. The person with the disc ("thrower") has ten seconds to throw the disc. The defender guarding the thrower ("marker") counts out the stall count.

When a pass in not completed (e.g. out of bounds, drop, block, interception), the defense immediately takes possession of the disc and becomes the offense.

Players not in the game may replace players in the game after a score and during an injury timeout. 3 substitutions (rolling) allowed per team.

No physical contact is allowed between players. Picks and screens are also prohibited. A foul occurs when contact is made.

Players are responsible for their own foul and line calls. Players resolve their own disputes.

Ultimate stresses sportsmanship and fair play. Competitive play is encouraged, but never at the expense of respect between players, adherence to the rules, and the basic joy of play.
